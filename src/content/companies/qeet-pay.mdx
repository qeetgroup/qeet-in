## Overview

Every Indian SaaS company, marketplace, and enterprise eventually wires the same stack: Razorpay for payments, Chargebee for billing, ClearTax for GST. Three vendors, three dashboards, three reconciliations, and financial data split across all of them. Every Qeet product faces the same problem internally. Qeet Pay collapses that into one API, one dashboard, and one integration — payment acceptance, payouts, subscription billing, GST-compliant invoicing, payment orchestration, fraud detection, embedded finance, and financial analytics.

No platform today combines UPI and NACH with subscription billing, GST e-invoicing (IRN), GSTR-1 auto-filing, marketplace GST split, AI dunning, and WhatsApp-native invoicing in a single product. Qeet Pay is built India-first, multi-tenant by construction, and on a double-entry ledger from day one.

## What makes it different

- **Payments and billing in one place** — UPI (collect, intent, AutoPay, and Credit Line on UPI), cards with RBI CoFT network tokenization, net banking, wallets, NACH, and virtual accounts, alongside a full subscription billing engine spanning flat-rate, per-unit, tiered, usage-based, and hybrid pricing.
- **GST compliance built in** — auto-calculated CGST/SGST/IGST, e-invoicing with IRN, GSTR-1 auto-filing, GSTR-2B reconciliation, and multi-GSTIN management. A combined QR carries IRP-validated e-invoice data and a UPI payment intent together, so a buyer scans once to verify the invoice and pay it.
- **AI where it pays back** — UPI-aware dunning that classifies payment failures and adapts channel and timing for materially higher recovery, ML payment orchestration that routes each transaction to the best provider, and explainable fraud scoring that returns the top signals behind every block in plain English.
- **WhatsApp-native** — invoice delivery, a subscription-management bot, dunning sequences, and payout confirmations, all over WhatsApp.
- **Built on the Qeet platform** — merchant and customer identity from Qeet ID, payment and dunning notifications through Qeet Notify, an immutable financial audit trail in qeet-logs, salary and statutory disbursement with Qeet People, and a dashboard and checkout SDK on Qeetrix.

## On the roadmap

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Q3–Q4 2026)**

Launching as a sub-merchant under a licensed payment aggregator — everything except direct acquiring.

- Payments: UPI, cards, net banking, and wallets
- Payouts: UPI, IMPS, and NEFT, including bulk disbursal with maker-checker approval
- Subscription billing: flat-rate, per-unit, and tiered, with basic dunning
- GST invoicing (pre-IRN)
- Go, TypeScript, Python, and Java SDKs, and a `qp` CLI
- Qeet ecosystem integration — Qeet ID, Qeet Notify, qeet-logs, and Qeet People payroll

**Phase 2 — Depth and intelligence (Q1–Q2 2027)**

- UPI 2.0, NACH, and virtual accounts
- AI dunning, ML payment orchestration, and automatic provider failover
- E-invoicing (IRN via the IRP), GSTR-1 auto-filing, and multi-GSTIN
- Usage-based billing, and marketplace split payments with GST attribution
- WhatsApp invoicing and bot, Account-Aggregator-powered embedded lending, and cash-flow forecasting

**Phase 3 — Enterprise and global (Q3–Q4 2027)**

- An own RBI payment-aggregator license, and PA-CB for cross-border payments
- UPI Lite and 123Pay, virtual card issuing, embedded insurance, and digital escrow
- Natural-language analytics, multi-region, ONDC integration, and SOC 2 Type II
